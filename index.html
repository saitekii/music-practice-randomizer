Music Practice Randomizer

A drilling partner for musicians. It throws random prompts at you — chords, scales, intervals, notes — and your job is to play them. That's the whole app. The randomness is the point: it forces you out of the comfortable keys and patterns you already know.

Getting started

Open Settings and choose what you want to drill — any combination of Chords, Scales, Intervals, Note Finder, Functional Harmony, and Diatonic Chords. Then hit Next Prompt or press Space. Play the prompt on your instrument. Hit Next again. Repeat.

The Root Notes section controls which of the 12 chromatic roots appear in prompts. Uncheck any you're not ready for yet.

Timer modes

  • Off — advance manually at your own pace. Good for slow, deliberate practice or when you're learning something new.
  • Countdown — auto-advances after a set number of seconds. Good for building speed and staying honest about whether you actually know something or just think you do.
  • Metronome — keeps a steady beat and advances on the bar. Dial in a tempo and practice staying in time.

MIDI & audio

Connect a MIDI keyboard, then click MIDI: On. The app listens to what you play and auto-advances the moment you get it right — no button press needed.

  • Visual keyboard — lights up as you play. Correct notes glow purple; wrong notes (notes not in the target chord or scale) flash red immediately.
  • ♪ Hear it — the small music-note button in the controls row plays the target through the built-in synth so you can hear what you're aiming for before you attempt it.
  • Synth presets — Rhodes, Organ, Pad, Bell, Pluck. Choose whatever sound keeps you engaged. Sustain pedal supported.
  • Response time — after each correct answer, your time flashes briefly. Running average and best time are shown below the MIDI controls.

MIDI requires Chrome or Edge. Other browsers don't support the Web MIDI API.

Learning Path

New to your instrument? The Learning Path walks you through 41 stages, starting from finding a single note and building step by step to seventh chords and modes. Each stage configures the app's settings exactly right for that skill level — you don't have to think about what to practice, just follow the stages.

Clicking Shuffle Settings exits the path and returns you to manual control.

Adaptive Practice

Enable this in Settings → Adaptive Practice. The app tracks how long each MIDI-confirmed answer takes, using an exponential moving average to score each root note and chord / scale type. Categories that take you longer show up more often; ones you've mastered fade back toward normal frequency.

Weighting activates after at least 3 confirmed answers per category, so initial data doesn't immediately skew anything. The EMA naturally self-corrects over time — a few bad answers won't permanently stack a category. Use Reset learning data to start fresh.

The philosophy

Most practice is too comfortable. You play what you already know, in the keys that feel natural, at a tempo that doesn't challenge you. This app makes that impossible by design.

The goal isn't to score points or complete levels. It's to shrink the list of things that feel hard. When Db Major 7 stops requiring a moment's thought and just comes out — that's the progress. There's no shortcut to it. You just have to do it a lot.
